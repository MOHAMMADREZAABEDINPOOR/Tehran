About Tehran

The story behind the personality, the journey, and the mission

Tehran "SoParvaz"

½ Black, ½ Persian, and ½ Amazing - these are the ingredients that make Tehran "SoParvaz" one of the most dynamic entertainer newcomers today.

Exploding on the scene hosting, performing, and throwing events from sold-out comedy shows to rock concerts, his energy, excitement, and unique sense of humor have made him a formidable comedian and entertainer in his own right. The 25-year-old sharp-witted Law student has been compared to a mix of Dave Chappelle and Stephen Colbert meet Maz Jobrani.

Hailing from the nation's capital, Washington D.C., Tehran was born and raised in the United States to an Iranian father and a Black-American mother. His parents chose the name Tehran as a reminder of his Persian roots and culture. The nickname "SoParvaz" - a more literal way of saying "So Fly" - was added much later. Speaking, reading, and writing Farsi, he has great respect for his Iranian heritage as well as his American side. Welcome, enjoy and know now and forever: I AM TEHRAN.

The Journey

From Washington DC to Hollywood's Biggest Stages

DC Roots & Cultural Identity

Born in Washington D.C. to an Iranian father and Black-American mother, Tehran's name was chosen as a reminder of his Persian roots. Growing up speaking, reading, and writing Farsi, he developed a deep respect for both his Iranian heritage and American upbringing, giving him the best of both worlds.

Law Student & Entertainer

As a sharp-witted Law student, Tehran balanced academics with his passion for entertainment. His legal background combined with natural comedic talent created a unique voice - often compared to a mix of Dave Chappelle, Stephen Colbert, and Maz Jobrani.

Breaking Through

From hosting highly-rated TV and radio programs to performing at sold-out comedy shows and rock concerts, Tehran exploded onto the scene. His energy, excitement, and "sheytoon" (mischievous) personality made him a formidable presence on Comedy Central, Def Comedy Jam, and beyond.

The "SoParvaz" Style

Tehran is definitely NO angel - in fact, he's very "sheytoon" (mischievous). Being in trouble and getting out of it is part of his charm. From going to school to going to court, Tehran talks openly about his life exploits and lessons on stage and TV, connecting with audiences of all walks of life.

Putting motivation and inspiration in his humor (or vice versa), Tehran encourages younger and older audiences alike to understand each other, respect each other, learn from each other, and teach each other. His comedy is real, relatable, and always from the heart.
